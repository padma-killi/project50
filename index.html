Interesting Facts about Camel:

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Interesting Facts about Chameleon:

  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar.
  • The change their color to communicate or to regulate body temperature.

Interesting Facts about Pigeon:

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.

Interesting Facts about Teddies:

  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt.
  • A teddy has been into space! Magellan T Bear boarded Space Shuttle Discovery in 1995 as part of a school project.
  • A person who is too fond of or loves collecting teddy bears is called an Arctophile.

Interesting Facts about Pandas:

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink(as babies) to white and black.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Interesting Facts about Cicadas:

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • There are over 3,390 varieties of cicadas around the world.
